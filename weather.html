Weather Analysis

This is an exploration of weather around the world based on a random set of latitude and longitudes. Is there a correlation when it comes to longitude and latitude with temperature, humidity, cloudiness and wind speed?

This review takes a look at random cities around the world to investigate the question.

Process of Analysis

  1. Generated a list of random latitude and longitude coordinates.
  2. Used Openweathermap.org API to find the nearest cities to the random list of latitude and longitude pairs.
  3. Received a JSON response of the weather details.
  4. Iterated over the JSON responses and pulled out the city information.
  5. City information: City, Latitude, Longitude, Temperature, Humidity, Cloudiness, and Wind Speed.
  6. Created a DataFrame of the data.

City Latitude vs Temperature

There's definitely a correlation between temperature and and latitude.

City Latitude vs Humidity

There's no correlation between Humidity and Latitude.

City Latitude vs Cloudiness

There's no correlation between Cloudiness and Latitude.

City Latitude vs Wind Speed

There's no correlation between Wind Speed and Latitude. In this case, there's no particular region that is more windy than others.

Observations

As one goes below the equator (zeroth circle of latitude), the temperature begins to decrease. Humidity, Cloudiness, and Wind Speed have no correlation with latitude.
